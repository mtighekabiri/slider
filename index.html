Brand-Led vs Influencer-Led Advertising

Exploring the spectrum between shallow-wide brand effects and narrow-deep influencer effects

The Theory: Attention vs Attribution

Brand-Led Advertising

Higher production quality with obvious brand cues and assets. People recognise these as adverts quickly, reducing raw attention time. However, the strong brand signals mean those who do engage can easily attribute the ad back to the brand — driving higher recall, consideration, and purchase intent across a broad audience.

Shallow but wide effects High recall Strong brand cues Higher production Broad reach

Influencer-Led Advertising

Often lower production quality, blending into organic content. People watch longer because it doesn't immediately register as an ad, generating strong attention metrics. But the lack of brand assets means fewer viewers connect the content back to the brand — creating deep engagement for a minority but weaker overall brand outcomes.

Narrow but deep effects High attention Low brand cues Organic feel Niche impact

The trade-off: Brand ads sacrifice attention for attribution. Influencer ads sacrifice attribution for attention. The slider below maps real examples across this spectrum. Even within each type, amplifiers like comedy, context, and relevance can boost effectiveness — these are independent levers that enhance either approach.
